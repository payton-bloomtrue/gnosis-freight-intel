Gnosis Freight Intel Brief

Week of June 9 – June 15, 2026  ·  Generated June 15, 2026

Executive Summary

  1. The early peak is now official. NRF/Hackett's June 9 Global Port Tracker forecasts 2.25M TEU into U.S. ports in June (+14.3% YoY), then sharp drops in July (–8.4%) and August (–8.6%) as importers frontload ahead of August tariff and fuel-cost increases.
  2. Spot rates kept climbing into June 15 — but slower. Xeneta shows Far East–USWC up 4.8% to $6,178/FEU and USEC up 3.9% to $7,114/FEU. The Iran/Red Sea conflict is the dominant driver (FE–USWC up 276% vs. mid-December).
  3. The booking window stretched from 2 weeks to 5. C.H. Robinson (Supply Chain Dive, June 12) says pull-forward has tripled the booking cycle — an execution-planning story, not just a rate story.
  4. AI is visibly displacing forwarding labor. Expeditors credited AI for productivity gains weeks before cutting 230 jobs (June 11), moving the "AI replaces manual logistics work" narrative into headlines.
  5. Amazon opened its LTL network nationwide (June 10–11), a reminder that platform players keep pushing deeper into multimodal freight execution.

Biggest Opportunities

Risks / Competitive Signals
