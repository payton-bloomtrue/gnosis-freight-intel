## Executive Summary

**Top 5 things happening this week:**

1. **project44 landed inside the SAP ecosystem — its biggest distribution move of the quarter.** On June 16, project44's Real-Time Multi-Modal Visibility Data solution became an SAP Endorsed App, purchasable directly on the SAP Store and feeding SAP Business Network for Logistics live carrier signal across ocean, rail, drayage, air, and parcel. This is a direct competitive signal: project44 is buying its way into enterprise procurement through the system Gnosis's largest BCO targets already run their supply chains on. Gnosis needs a crisp answer to "why us vs. the SAP-endorsed option" for any account standardized on SAP.

2. **The Strait of Hormuz reopening became real this week — but the rate relief importers are hoping for is months away.** An interim US-Iran deal was signed June 17 (the "Islamabad Declaration," formal ratification slated for June 19 in Geneva), the strait is expected to reopen within ~30 days, and the first Saudi-flagged tankers transited on June 18. The carrier reality is far slower: Maersk, MSC, CMA CGM, Hapag-Lloyd, and MOL all signaled they will *not* resume normal transit until safety is proven on the water, keeping Cape of Good Hope routing — and its $100–180/TEU cost premium and 34–38 day transit times — in place. For Gnosis's ICP this is the headline: a "peace deal" does not equal restored ETAs, and planning on the assumption that it does is dangerous.

3. **Peak season is firmly early and rates are still climbing — carriers are fully booked into July.** Drewry's World Container Index climbed again into June 18 as early peak-season demand kept pricing firm, and Xeneta's latest update has Far East–USWC up 127% versus pre-crisis levels with carriers reporting services fully booked into July — even large shippers on valid long-term contracts can't get space. The pull-forward is tariff- and fuel-driven (importers racing ahead of expected August cost increases), with World Cup cargo adding volume. This is a live, high-urgency execution moment for every enterprise importer Gnosis sells to.

4. **The bottleneck is moving from the port to everything after the port.** C.H. Robinson's June freight market update frames inland coordination — rail variability, uneven container arrivals, trucking/drayage constraints, schedule changes — as the larger planning challenge now, not terminal congestion itself. That is precisely the "execution beyond visibility" story Gnosis is built to own: knowing where the box is matters less than orchestrating the rail/drayage/inland legs once it lands.

5. **Transshipment-hub congestion (Singapore, Port Klang) is the under-covered second-order disruption.** Both the gCaptain reopening analysis and Xeneta call out that Hormuz workarounds have jammed major Southeast Asian transshipment hubs, and that "port disruption is toxic for supply chains" when it hits hubs of global significance. This congestion is rippling into Transpacific lanes with no direct Middle East exposure — a useful, less-obvious angle for Gnosis content.

**Biggest opportunities for Gnosis this week:**
- The "Hormuz reopening ≠ rates normalizing" gap is the single sharpest narrative of the week — importers want relief; the data says don't bet on it yet. Gnosis can be the voice of operational realism.
- C.H. Robinson's "inland is now the hard part" framing is a ready-made wedge for Gnosis's execution-and-orchestration positioning vs. visibility-only competitors.
- "Fully booked into July" + tariff pull-forward = an active buying trigger for enterprise importers feeling the pain right now.

**Biggest risks / competitive signals:**
- **project44's SAP Endorsed App is a structural distribution threat**, not just a feature drop — it embeds a competitor in the procurement and data fabric of Gnosis's largest enterprise targets. This needs a sales-enablement response, not just a content response.
- Rates are still rising, but the reopening headlines may start to soften urgency in buyers' minds even though carriers haven't actually returned — capitalize on the peak-season + "premature relief" narrative now, before sentiment shifts.
